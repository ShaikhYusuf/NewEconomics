Lesson 5: Need and Objectives of Education Management

Objectives

  • Explain the concept of education management.
  • Describe the need for educational management.
  • Identify the objectives of educational management.

Concept of Education Management

Understanding Education Management: Educational management refers to the systematic planning, organizing, directing, and controlling of all activities and resources within educational institutions to achieve educational objectives effectively. It involves optimizing human and material resources to enhance teaching, research, and community engagement while fostering institutional growth and societal development.

Dynamic Nature of Educational Institutions: Educational institutions operate in a constantly changing environment influenced by:

  • Technological Advancements: The integration of modern tools and methods in the teaching-learning process.
  • Policy Changes: New governmental rules, regulations, and policies impact operations.
  • Societal Needs: Adjustments required to meet evolving societal expectations and demands.

Due to these factors, educational institutions must continuously improve internal processes and adapt to changes to maintain relevance and effectiveness.

Origin and Development: Educational management originated in the United States in the early 20th century as a specialized field. The United Kingdom adopted this concept in the 1960s, emphasizing structured management in educational settings.

Definitions:

Henry Fayol’s Definition of Management: “To manage is to forecast and plan, to organize, to command, to co-ordinate, and to control.”

Management involves a systematic process comprising five core functions: planning, organizing, commanding, coordinating, and controlling.

Definition of Educational Management: Educational management applies Fayol’s principles to educational settings, focusing on human resources, material resources, regulations, policies, and societal integration.

Need for Educational Management

Educational management is crucial for ensuring that educational institutions operate effectively, especially in a dynamic and resource-constrained environment. Key reasons include:

  • Adapting to changes in government policies and evolving societal needs.
  • Optimizing resource utilization (human and material).
  • Addressing regulatory constraints.
  • Meeting societal needs and developing a skilled workforce.
  • Enhancing the institutional environment to motivate and engage staff.
  • Promoting professional growth and improving quality of education.
  • Proactively planning for future needs and ensuring sustainability.
  • Strengthening community relationships and preparing for competition.
  • Ensuring equity and accessibility in education.
  • Managing interpersonal conflicts and maintaining harmony.

Objectives of Educational Management

  • Achievement of institutional objectives by aligning goals.
  • Enhancing planning, organizing, and implementation processes.
  • Creating and maintaining a positive public image.
  • Optimal utilization of human and material resources.
  • Enhancing institutional efficiency and performance monitoring.
  • Preventing work duplication and improving workflow.
  • Conflict management and promoting collaboration.
  • Promoting qualitative education and maintaining discipline with flexibility.
  • Strengthening human relationships among stakeholders.
